Management 2 mins

Understanding refunds

📋 This guide is for Just Eat partners who use Just Eat drivers. If you deliver yourself, click here.

We know refunds are a concern for partners. You've told us you need more transparency about how our refund process works and the steps we've taken to ensure fairness.

We've produced a new guide that explains our systems, protections, and best practices to help you reduce refund-related costs. Download the full guide below, or read the summary here.

How refunds are processed

You're only charged for refunds when the issue is within your control. For orders where Just Eat completed delivery, we cover delivery-related refunds. Issues like missing items, incorrect orders, or food safety concerns are your responsibility, while courier-related problems are ours.

Refund responsibility
Issue typeExamplesWho is charged
Food quality / accuracyMissing items, incorrect orders, food safety concernsPartner
Delivery — partner courierCourier issues when you self-deliverPartner
💡 You are charged for these refunds because the issue falls within your control. Review our best practices below to reduce your exposure.

Confirmed fraudFalse claims detected by our systemNobody — reversed
✅ You are not charged for these refunds. Just Eat absorbs delivery-related costs and automatically reverses confirmed fraudulent claims.
Fraud protection: built to protect your margins

Our refund process balances customer protection with partner fairness. When a customer reports an issue, every claim passes through our multi-layered fraud prevention system. High-risk cases are escalated to our specialist fraud investigation team for manual review.

Our data-led fraud prevention system uses sophisticated checks across five signal categories. Confirmed fraud is blocked, reversed, and fed back into our detection models to improve accuracy continuously.

1
Customer identity & behavioural analytics
Flags unusual account activity and patterns
2
Order patterns
Cross-references order history against claim frequency
3
Refund velocity
Detects customers making excessive refund requests
4
Photo evidence
Customers must submit visual proof for certain claims
5
GPS trails & chat logs
Delivery route and communication records used to verify or dispute claims

Best practices to reduce refunds

Small operational changes can significantly reduce your refund exposure. The table below outlines recommended actions by category, each rated by its likely impact on your refund rate.

CategoryRecommended actionImpact
Menu accuracyUse real photos and accurate descriptions on your menuHigh
Stock managementMark items out of stock promptly to avoid substitution disputesHigh
Menu structureBuild combo meals to show sub-items for clearer refund allocationMedium
Packaging — temperaturePackage cold items separately from hot food to prevent damageMedium
Packaging — bulky itemsEnsure drivers collect bulky items left outside main packagingMedium
